Stephen Hawking

Cosmologist, space traveller and hero

The physicist Stephen Hawking in 1979 in Princeton, New Jersey

Here's a timeline of Stephen Hawkings life:

1942 Born on 8 January
1952 Attended St Albans School after passing the eleven-plus a year early.
1959 Began his university education at University College, Oxford
1962 Received a first-class BA (Hons.) degree in natural science and began his graduate work at Trinity Hall, Cambridge
1963 Diagnosed with motor neurone disease
1966 Published his PhD thesis "Properties of Expanding Universes" and awarded Adams Prize for his essay "Singularities and the Geometry of Space-Time"
1969 Accepted a specially created Fellowship for Distinction in Science to remain at Caius.
1970 Postulated what became known as the second law of black hole dynamics. Stating that the event horizon of a black hole can never get smaller.
1971 Won the Gravity Research Foundation Award with his essay titled "Black Holes"
1973 Published first book, The Large Scale Structure of Space-Time
1974 Discovered Hawking radiation
1975 Awarded both the Eddington Medal and the Pius XI Gold Medal
1976 Awarded the Dannie Heineman Prize, the Maxwell Prize and the Hughes Medal
1977 Appointed a professor with a chair in gravitational physics
1978 Received the Albert Einstein Medal and an honorary doctorate from the University of Oxford
1979 Elected Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at the University of Cambridge
1982 Awarded the American Franklin Medal and appointed a Commander of the Order of the British Empire (CBE)
1985 Received the Gold Medal of the Royal Astronomical Society
1987 Awarded the Paul Dirac Medal
1988 Published "A Brief History of Time" and also awarded the Wolf Prize
1989 Appointed a Companion of Honour (CH)
1996 Published a series of lectures as "The Nature of Space and Time"
1992 Premiere of a film version of A Brief History of Time
1997 A six-part television series "Stephen Hawking's Universe" and a companion book were released
2001 Published "The Universe in a Nutshell"
2005 Published "A Briefer History of Time" which aimed to make his previous works accessible to a wider audience
2006 Published "God Created the Integers" and awarded the Copley Medal from the Royal Society
2007 With his daughter Lucy published "George's Secret Key to the Universe", a children's book designed to explain theoretical physics
2008 Awarded the Fonseca Prize
2009 Awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom, which is America's highest civilian honour
2013 Awarded the Russian Special Fundamental Physics Prize
2017 Awarded an Honorary Doctorate from Imperial College London
2018 Stephen Hawking died peacefully at his home in Cambridge on 14 March 2018 at the age of 76 from complications

"Look up at the stars and not down at your feet. Try to make sense of what you see, and wonder about what makes the universe exist. Be curious."

-Stephen Hawking
